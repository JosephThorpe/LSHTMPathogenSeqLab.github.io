titleForBox: A new study highlights the utility of sequencing and informatics in investigating cryptic malaria transmission

brief: Using a combination of Illumina and Oxford Nanopore sequencing platforms alongside the Malaria-Profiler informatics tool, we rapidly profiled parasites from four cryptic <i>Plasmodium falciparum</i> cases in the UK. This approach enabled the identification of drug resistance markers and the prediction of geographic origins using AI-based methods. We further applied whole-genome sequencing to family-linked clusters of <i>Plasmodium ovale spp.</i> and <i>Plasmodium falciparum</i> cases, confirming (sub)species identities and supporting fine-scale transmission cluster analysis. Overall, this study demonstrates the power of real-time whole-genome sequencing coupled with AI-enhanced analytics for high-resolution malaria genomic surveillance.
