brief: We generated the first chromosome-scale genome assembly for <i>Dermacentor reticulatus<i>, a key European tick vector, using Oxford Nanopore sequencing; the repeat-rich genome (63.6% repetitive sequences) achieved 97.9% BUSCO completeness with 21,592 annotated genes enriched in functions related to detoxification, antimicrobial defence, and cholesterol metabolism. This genomic resource provides a foundation for investigating tick biology, evolution, and host-pathogen interactions relevant to disease transmission.
